# How to Choose an HNS Mining Pool

A guide for miners entering the Handshake ecosystem.

---

## The Most Important Question

> **"Will this pool still be here when HNS price is low?"**

Any pool can operate when mining is profitable. The real test of a pool is whether they stay during bear markets. This repository exists to answer that question with facts.

---

## What to Look For in a Pool

### 1. Track Record Through Bear Markets
Check this repository. Has the pool exited HNS mining before during low price periods? A pool that abandoned miners once is likely to do it again.

### 2. HNS Dedication vs Multi-Coin Pools
- **HNS-dedicated pools** (like 6block) have their entire business tied to HNS. They cannot easily "switch" to another coin. Their incentives align with yours.
- **Multi-coin pools** (like F2Pool, ViaBTC) can redirect miner resources to more profitable coins when HNS underperforms. Some do this without warning.

This doesn't mean multi-coin pools are bad — F2Pool has been loyal to HNS. But it's a risk factor worth knowing.

### 3. Communication
Did the pool announce changes in advance? Did they give miners time to migrate? Check the pool's record in this repository for how they handled past exits or disruptions.

### 4. Fee Structure
Lower fees are better, but not at the expense of reliability. A pool with slightly higher fees that never abandons you is worth more than a cheap pool that exits in a bear market.

### 5. Payout Threshold & Method
- PPS (Pay Per Share) — you get paid per valid share, even if the pool doesn't find a block. More stable income.
- PPLNS (Pay Per Last N Shares) — earnings tied to actual blocks found. More variance but can be higher over time.

## Red Flags

- Pool exited HNS before without notice
- No public announcement channels (Discord, Twitter, Telegram)
- No verifiable team or company behind the pool
- Payouts have been delayed without explanation in the past
- No response to miner support requests

## How to Migrate Pools Safely

If you're moving away from an unreliable pool:

1. Wait until your current balance exceeds the payout threshold, then request a manual payout if available
2. Point your miners to the new pool stratum address
3. Confirm shares are being accepted on the new pool dashboard before removing old config
4. Keep the old pool config saved in case you need to revert
